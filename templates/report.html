Clear Thinking Thinking plays a vital role in human species. Thinking can be defined as a process or action of using one's mind to produce thought. These thoughts form the basis of various human transactions like planning, problem solving, decision making, communication etc. Clear Thinking means the person is able to identify similarity, identical and differences scientifically. The person with clear thinking normally eliminates mistakes while doing own work.
Observational Abilities Observation means to closely monitor something or someone. It refers to the ability of the individual to utilize all their five senses to recognize, analyze and recall the surroundings. This skill employs the person with varied information about any object, event, attitude or phenomenon, using one or more senses at a time. A correct observation extends data, scores or values to give scientific meaning which can be true about data and values.
Reasoning Abilities Reasoning means evaluating the claim in respect to their supporting arguments and evidence. The crucial aspect of reasoning ability is generation and maintenance of the viewpoints or beliefs that are conforming to, and justified with relevant knowledge. Reasoning is a process of thinking that evaluates a situation in a logical manner. Reasoning is most useful in higher order jobs and for assessment of various situations in life.
Critical Reasoning With this ability, a person is able to identify principle or hypothesis in a given situation. Critical reasoning allows a person to give the correct reason and accept only the correct answer in difficult situations also. It guides on deciding what to believe in. It involves identifying, analyzing the issue and fixing the flaws, so as to adapt the situation. Critical reasoning is organization of thoughts in a rational manner; to understand the connection between the ideas and facts of the information Page given.
Abstract Reasoning Abstract thinking is the ability to understand the concepts which are real but they are not attached or tied directly to concrete physical objects and experience. It can be understood as the ability to absorb information from the senses and make connections to the wider world. Abstract reasoning has three important components like Ideation, Imagination and creativity. All these three together work on abstract concepts effectively, hence called the inevitability of an individual.

Planning Skills Planning involves two key tasks: deciding what you need to learn, and then deciding how you are going to learn that material.
Students can ask questions to themselves - What are the Learning Objectives for this class? / What do I already know about this topic? / What are the concepts I need to master before my next test? / What do I want to learn about this topic?/What active learning strategies will support my learning?,etc
Implementation Skills Implementation is the carrying out, execution, or practice of a plan, a method, or any design, idea, model, specification, standard or policy for doing something. For an implementation process to be successful, many tasks between different departments need to be accomplished in sequence.
Be S.M.A.R.T. While most improvement plans have a series of goals, it's important to make sure the goals are SMART- Specific, Measurable, Actionable, Results-driven, and Time-bound.
Monitoring Skills Monitoring requires you to ask "how am I doing at learning this?". In monitoring, you are constantly tracking what you have learned, what you don't yet know, and whether your study strategies are helping you to learn effectively
Students can ask questions to themselves -What concepts do I understand well?/ What concepts are still confusing for me?/ Can I explain the material to someone else without referring to notes?/ Can I create and answer self-testing questions about these concepts?/ What other strategies could I use to learn this material?/ How can I make this material more personally relevant to me?, etc
Evaluation Skills Evaluation involves reflection on how well you met your Learning Objectives after completing a unit of study, or receiving feedback (such as a test or assignment).
Students can ask questions to themselves -To what extent did I meet the Learning Objectives for this unit?/ What in my exam preparation worked well?/ What in my exam preparation did not go well?/ What do I want to change?/ How did my exam answer compare with the suggested answer? What key components did I miss? How will what I have learned help me in my next courses?
